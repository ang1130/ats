Abstract—With the surge of traffic and types carried by the 
network, the possibility of congestion in the network is 
increasing. However, the traditional queue scheduling 
algorithm has been unable to meet the network quality of 
service (QoS). Aiming at this situation, based on the analysis 
of token bucket algorithm and various queue scheduling 
algorithms, an improved token bucket and weighted fair 
queue (WFQ) scheduling algorithm is proposed. Simulation 
experiments show that the algorithm can effectively carry out 
flow control and flow shaping. At the same time, by adjusting 
the weight of the queue length, it will guarantee the network 
quality of service of the real-time data flow and improve the 
performance of network delay, jitter and data packet loss rate. 
Keywords—token bucket; weighted fair queue; quality of 
service; time delay; improved algorithm 
I. INTRODUCTION  
With the popularity of the Internet and the fast 
development of network technology, the network has 
penetrated into all aspects of people's work and life, 
becoming an indispensable part of people's work and life. 
The 50th Statistical Report [1] on China's Internet 
Development released by China Internet Network 
Information Center shows that as of June 2022, the netizens 
in China has reached 1.051 billion, and the Internet 
penetration rate has reached 74.4%. At the same time, in the 
first half of 2022 alone, China 's mobile Internet access 
traffic has reached 124.1 billion GB, with a 20.2 % year-
on-year growth. Especially since the outbreak of the global 
novel coronavirus pneumonia in 2020, real-time data flows 
such as video conferencing, online teaching, and home 
office have flourished. Network congestion, even network 
paralysis, has seriously affected the network quality of 
services. However, the most direct and effective way to 
improve the quality of network services is to increase the 
bandwidth of network links. But, network bandwidth 
resources are limited. Therefore, researchers solve the 
problem of network quality of service by combining token 
bucket technology and queue scheduling technology. In 
Reference [2], a WFQ scheduling algorithm based on 
service classification and bandwidth allocation was 
designed, which not only maintains the fairness of WFQ 
algorithm but also meets the requirements of the quality of 
service for real-time services. A weighted fair queue 
scheduling algorithm with weight adjustment was proposed 
in literature [3][4][5][6]. The delay and packet loss rate of 
important services are reduced, and the QoS of real-time 
services is improved. Reference [7] implements a quality of 
service-oriented SDN global multi-channel runoff 
scheduling algorithm, which alleviated the problems of 
network congestion and large delay. Literature [8] reduced 
the bandwidth fragmentation and guaranteed the quality of 
service of small flows by optimizing the First Meet the 
Bandwidth Algorithm (FMBA), and improved the network 
throughput by establishing the weight function. By 
dynamically assigning weights and adjusting the service 
order of the queue, the literature [9] reduced the delay and 
packet loss, and met the quality of service requirements of 
different types of data in different self-similarity degrees. In 
literature [10][11], the PQ + WFQ scheduling mechanism 
is applied to the IP service network, which not only 
guarantees the quality of service of real-time services, but 
also avoids the phenomenon of " starvation " of low-latency 
services. 
In order to improve the QoS, an improved scheduling 
algorithm based on token bucket and WFQ is proposed on 
the basis of summarizing and analyzing the existing 
literature. In this algorithm, token buckets is used for flow 
control and traffic shaping. At the same time, by adjusting 
the weight of queue length, the network service quality of 
real-time services is guaranteed, and the network delay, 
jitter and data packet loss rate are improved.

II. THE PRINCIPLE OF WFQ ALGORITHM 
The WFQ model [12][13] is an approximation 
algorithm of the Generalized Processor Sharing ( GPS ) 
model, which records the time of each data packet from the 
start of entering the queue to the next node ( called virtual 
completion time ), and then the scheduler forwards the data 
packet based on the minimum virtual completion time. 
When the data packet enters the queue, the server calculates 
the virtual completion time of each data packet, then adds 
the virtual completion time label to the data packet and puts 
it into the queue. When the scheduler schedules data 
packets, the server queries whether there are data packets in 
the queue. If it does not continue to wait for data packets to 
arrive, it will query and forward the data packets with the 
minimum virtual completion time, and update the virtual 
time function. 
In the WFQ algorithm, it is assumed that there are N 
queues to be served, and the weight of each queue is wi 
(i=1,2,3……N). If the service rate of the scheduler is r and

service objects in this time period. If the virtual time 
function is defined as V(t), V(t) is set to 0 when the 
scheduler is idle, and the set time starts from time 0, that is 
V(0) = 0, then the virtual time at time t

The WFQ algorithm sets weights for each queue, and its 
scheduler can schedule each queue fairly so that all queues 
can be served. This avoids the phenomenon of ' starving ' in 
non-real-time services. However, because the weight of 
each queue in the WFQ algorithm cannot be adjusted after 
being set, if the weight of a queue is large and the number 
of data packets in the queue is small, it will cause a waste 
of bandwidth resources. At the same time, if the weight of 
a queue is small and the number of data packets in the queue 
is large, congestion is prone to occur, resulting in increased 
data packet jitter, delay, and data packet loss rate.

improved scheduling algorithm based on token bucket and 
weighted fair queue is proposed. The algorithm controls the 
flow and traffic shaping of burst flow and data packet 
through token bucket. At the same time, the network QoS 
of real-time services is ensured by adjusting the queue 
weight strategy. The improved principle based on token

When the data packet is divided into N queues by the 
classifier shown in Figure 2, the data packet in the queue is 
allowed to flow into the token bucket. After filtering by the 
token bucket, the bursty service or the data packet with too 
large average rate is smoothly transitioned, and then the 
processed data packet is input into the queue adjuster. The 
queue length is adjusted by the queue adjuster, and finally 
the data packet is forwarded by the scheduler. The 
algorithm controls the traffic well, solves the problem that 
some data packets can not get bandwidth resources for a 
long time or some data packets occupy too much bandwidth 
resources, and improves the QoS of the whole network. 
A. The Design of Token Bucket  
As shown in Figure 2, in order to limit burst traffic, 
achieve traffic control and shaping, avoid network 
congestion, and improve network QoS, a token bucket is 
added to the WFQ algorithm schematic diagram shown in 
Figure 1. Token bucket [14] is a network model with traffic 
and rate constraints, which controls data traffic by the 
number of tokens in the token bucket. The schematic 
diagram is shown in Figure 3. 
 
Figure 3. The Principle of Token Bucket Algorithm 
Assuming that the token generation rate is rtoken, that 
is, rtoken tokens are injected into the bucket per second. If 
the maximum number of tokens that can be accommodated 
in the token bucket is P, the working principle of the token 
bucket [12] [15] [16] is described as follows: 
The tokens are injected into the bucket at a constant rate 
rtoken. When the number of tokens in the bucket is P, it 
indicates that the token bucket is full, and the newly 
generated token will be discarded. When the data packet 
arrives, because the forwarding data packet needs to obtain 
a token, and the number of tokens in the token bucket needs 
to be greater than the number of data packets before it can 
be forwarded and transmitted. After the data packet obtains 
the token from the token bucket, the number of tokens in 
the bucket will decrease. If the number of tokens in the 
bucket is insufficient, the data packet will be stored in the 
buffer. At this time, it is necessary to wait for the increase 
of the number of tokens in the bucket until there are enough 
tokens in the bucket to be forwarded and transmitted. If the 
data packets in the buffer exceed the capacity of the buffer, 
the new data packets will be discarded.  
The token bucket algorithm controls the data 
forwarding transmission rate and smoothes the burst traffic. 
As long as there are enough tokens in the bucket, a certain 
degree of data traffic burst can be smoothed.

By analyzing Figure 7 and Figure 8, it can be seen that 
when sudden traffic flows appear in the network, the time 
delay of video traffic flow and voice traffic flow is greatly 
increased by using the weighted fair queuing algorithm, and 
it takes about 3 seconds to recover to the steady state. But, the 
improved algorithm controls the flow of the burst flow 
through the token bucket and the delay change is not obvious. 
This indicates that the improved algorithm based on token 
bucket and weighted fair queue scheduling has better network 
quality of service. 
Through testing and calculation, in the absence of sudden 
traffic flow, the algorithm based on token bucket and WFQ 
scheduling is improved to reduce the average data packet loss 
rate of each service. The average data packet loss rate of real-
time video data stream and real-time voice data stream is 
close to 0.02%, while that of non-real-time traffic stream is 
close to 0.1%. When there are bursty services, the average 
data packet loss rate of real-time video data stream and real-
time voice data stream is close to 0.05%, while the average 
data packet loss rate of non-real-time services is about 0.15%. 
It can be seen that, compared with the weighted fair queue 
algorithm, the improved algorithm based on token bucket and 
WFQ scheduling distinguishes real-time traffic, and the delay, 
jitter and data packet loss rate of real-time traffic have been 
effectively improved, thus improving the QoS of the network. 
V. C
ONCLUSION 
On the basis of the WFQ algorithm, an improved 
algorithm based on token bucket and WFQ scheduling is 
proposed. The simulation results show that the introduction 
of token bucket has a good effect on smoothing the burst 
traffic, and the improved WFQ algorithm reduces the delay of 
real-time traffic in the network. The average delay is less than 
70 ms in the case of insufficient link bandwidth resources. In 
the case of sudden traffic, the delay changes very little. At the 
same time, the network data packet loss rate is also reduced. 
Under normal circumstances, the data packet loss rate of real-
time services is close to 0.02%, and the average data packet 
loss rate of non-real-time services is close to 0.1%.In the case 
of bursty services, the data packet loss rate of real-time 
services is close to 0.05%, and the average data packet loss 
rate of non-real-time services is about 0.15%. It has shown 
that the improvement of the scheduling algorithm based on 
token bucket and WFQ has effectively improves the network 
service quality.
